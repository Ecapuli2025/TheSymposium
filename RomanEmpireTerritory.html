A Map of the Roman Empire's Expansion... and Fall

By ThatPyramidBuilder

AT THE END OF THE 4TH CENTURY AD, the Western Roman Empire began to collapse. Many factors contributed to its collapse such as economic problems, bad emperors, barbarian invasions, and many more. The eastern part known as the Byzantine Empire would survive a millenium longer, however, eventually falling with the Ottoman capture of Constantinople. The painting in the picture above is Thomas Cole's "Destruction," the fourth piece in his "The Course of Empire," a series of paintings detailing civilization.
